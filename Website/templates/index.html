Presidential Elections

There is nothing that says democracy more than the word ELECTION!
For Project 3 of the Data Analytics Bootcamp, we chose to review presidential election results for the 2008, 2012, and 2016 election years by diving into the data for every county in the United States. In addition to election data, we collected education, median income, gross domestic product, and demographic (race, population, age, marital status, household size, military service, etc.), information for each county. Below we discuss our findings.

The Data

Collection, Cleaning, and Transforming

We gathered census and demographic data for each County in the United States. Together, the data sets covered the years 2008 to 2018, with a special focus on 2008, 2012, and 2016: the election years. Each data set was cleaned uniformly and grouped by County then by year before being aggregated in a master data set. Finally, we visualized the data to explore the possible connections between these many factors and the eventual outcome of each election, with the hope of one day accurately predicting election outcomes.
